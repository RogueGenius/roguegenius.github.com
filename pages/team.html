George Chamales

George is a security expert with over a decade of experience in the defense of computer systems. His security work includes vulnerability assessments of nationally significant critical infrastructure systems, development of new techniques to mitigate the threat of malicious hackers and evaluating the security of international companies. His work in the humanitarian sector includes supporting the development and deployment of software to collect information from citizens on the ground during natural disasters and times of crisis.

George can be reached at george@roguegenius.com.

Antonio Lettieri

Antonio is a Web Developer currently living in San Francisco, CA. He has specialized in corporate & freelance web development and consulting throughout his career. Antonio's contributions to the international development space began in 2010 shortly after the earthquake in Haiti. He has developed and contributed to a variety of different projects in the open source humanitarian space including plugins for the Ushahidi web platform. He has provide technical development support to deployments around the world. His work with Project RED's Join RED project was included in the 2011 Webby Awards.

Antonio can be reached at alettieri@gmail.com.

John Etherton (Alumni)

John is a programmer based out of Denver, CO, USA. He's been programming for over 10 years, and holds both a master's and a bachelor's degree in computer science from the Georgia Institute of Technology. While living in Liberia from 2008 to 2010 John became involved with Ushahidi. Since then he's worked on the Ushahidi Liberia project, and the maps for Japan, Somalia, and Libya. Some of John's contributions to the Ushahidi platform have been the Admin Map, Location Highlighter, and Simple Groups plugins, and various contributions to the Ushahidi core platform, focusing on improving quality. After living in the field for 3 years John is very aware of what does, and does not work on the ground. John is also active in the Open Data Kit (ODK) community working on the KoBo ODK variant and making contributions to the JavaRosa project. He created the DoForms plugin which feeds data from the DoForms ODK variant into an Ushahidi instance. John is the Managing Partner of Etherton Technologies, Ltd., and co-founder of iLab Liberia.

John can be reached at john@ethertontech.com.
